This page describes how our Group applies The Scout Association's
safeguarding rules. It is **not** a substitute for those rules - the
authoritative policy is published by The Scout Association, and this page
links to it rather than reproducing it.

## We follow Scouts UK safeguarding

We are a member group of The Scout Association. Every leader is
DBS-checked (or holds the host-country equivalent where appropriate),
trained, and operates under the Association's safeguarding policy and the
"Yellow Card" safe-from-harm rules at every meeting and camp.

- The Scout Association's safeguarding policy and the Yellow Card:
  <https://www.scouts.org.uk/safeguarding/> *(confirm the current link
  before publishing).*
- The rules of Scouting are set out in Policy, Organisation and Rules
  (POR): <https://www.scouts.org.uk/por/>

## Reporting a concern

If you are worried about the safety or wellbeing of a young person,
contact our Group Lead Volunteer straight away. If a child is in
immediate danger, call the emergency services. You can also raise a
concern directly with the Scouts Safeguarding Team - see the link above.
