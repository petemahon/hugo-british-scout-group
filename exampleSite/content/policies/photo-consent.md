## Why we ask

Some of what we do is naturally photogenic — camps, badge presentations,
parades. Sharing photos helps families see what their child has been
up to and shows prospective members what life in the Group is like.
We also have a duty of care to the young people in those photos.

## What we ask of parents and carers

When a young person joins, you'll be asked to tick a photo-consent
box on their OSM record. You can change your answer at any time:
contact the Group Lead Volunteer and we will update the record and
remove any already-published photos that include your child.

## How we use photos

- **Our website.** Galleries on this site are reviewed by the
  safeguarding lead before publication. Captions never identify a
  young person by name.
- **Group communications.** Newsletters and Group-wide messages on
  the parents' channel use the same consent setting.
- **Scouts UK and District.** Occasionally District or Headquarters
  ask for photos for their own publications. We tell them which
  records have consent and only forward those.

## What we don't do

- We don't post photos on social media platforms that we don't own
  (no Facebook, Instagram or TikTok presence for this Group).
- We don't sell photos.
- We don't embed third-party tracking on this site.

## Right to be forgotten

Email the Group Lead Volunteer and we'll remove any photo at your
request, no questions asked. Removal usually takes effect within
two working days.

## Where signed records are kept

Signed photo-consent records are stored against each young person's
record in OSM. Paper consent forms from camps are kept by the camp
leader for the duration of the activity and then logged in OSM.
