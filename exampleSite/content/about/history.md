We started in 1974 with six children and two borrowed trestle tables.
A pair of parents who had run Scout sections back in the UK found
themselves overseas, far from the nearest Group, and decided their own
children — and their friends — shouldn't miss out. The first meetings
ran in a villa majlis; within a few years we had affiliated to British
Scouting Overseas and were running the same programme, and working
towards the same awards, as Groups at home.

Fifty years on, the shape of the Group has changed many times — new
sections, new leaders, a permanent home — but the thread has held: the
same Skills for Life programme, delivered by volunteers, for the
children of families who happen to be living away from the UK.

## What's stayed the same

The desert camp. Since the first overnight in 1983, a night under the
stars in the dunes has been the fixture the whole Group builds its year
around — Squirrels watching the sunset, Explorers cooking for everyone,
and leaders who first came as Beavers.

## What's changed

Families move on — that's the nature of an overseas Group. Members we
waved off have gone on to lead Scouting on four continents, and every
anniversary brings a few of them back. The timeline alongside picks out
the moments that got us here.
