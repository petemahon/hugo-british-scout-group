Who can join

BSO is a slice of UK Scouting, here for families living in the United Arab Emirates. Whether you've moved here from the UK or somewhere else, our doors are open.

Every country has its own home of Scouting. Here, that's Emirates Scout Association — and if you're a national of the United Arab Emirates, they'd love to have you.

Volunteering's a different story. Anyone over 18 is welcome to help out, whatever your nationality — including nationals of the United Arab Emirates.

(This follows POR 3.2.1.1 — The Scout Association's rule for every BSO group.)

Frequently asked questions

What does a typical meeting look like? ›

Meetings run from arrival through investiture-style opening to a mix of games, badge work and outdoor activity, then a closing circle. Squirrels and Beavers run shorter sessions; Cubs, Scouts and Explorers run longer. Specific timings per section are on the cards above.

Most weeks we’re indoors at the hut, but we head outside whenever the weather and programme allow. Patrol/Six leaders run smaller groups within the meeting.

Do I have to volunteer to get my child a place? ›

No — but it helps. We have a long-standing principle that families who join in get priority on the waiting list. There’s a role for every amount of time you can give, from washing-up after camp to running a section. See the volunteer page for what we need.

Many parents volunteer for one or two terms while their child is settling in. There’s no expectation it becomes a long-term commitment unless you want it to.

Can my child come to a try-it session first? ›

Yes — every section runs a few trial weeks at the start of each term. Once you’re on the waiting list, the section leader will be in touch with try-it dates as places come up.

We don’t run open evenings as a rule — section meetings are busy and better suited to one child at a time joining in for a few weeks before formally enrolling.
